Project Management Context

Project management comprises the following core areas and activities :
  • Scoping, that is defining the desired project outcome, identifying the stakeholders, and setting the boundary conditions
  • Planning, that is creating a set of tasks, assigning resources to them, and ordering them along the timeline
  • Executing, that is to work on the tasks and finish them according to the plan, and to deal with any issues
  • Monitoring, that is to watch execution and notice any deviations from the plan as early as possible
  • Closing, that is to sum up project experiences and draw conclusions towards improved processes


Running a project means to deal with project management proper, quality management, risk management, and supply management.

Track+ supports project managers in a superb way in the scoping, executing, monitoring, and closing activities. Track+ supports project managers well in planning activities with lean functionality with regard to resource planning and scheduling.

TRACK offers features that make it a perfect fit for hybrid project management, that is project management encompassing the classic waterfall method and Scrum in the same project. This makes TRACK a first choice tool in managing all projects that consist of hardware and software.

Project management with Track+ is aimed towards collaboration. Team members are encouraged to be engaged in project management, to plan their activities in detail and to update work progress in real-time.

This guide focuses on planning, executing, monitoring, and closing projects with Track+. For scoping and quality management please refer to the requirements and test module documentation.

This guide shall help you to configure your system for your project management needs and to show you how to use Track+ for managing your projects.
