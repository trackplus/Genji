Planning

Track+ permits you to plan dates, efforts and cost for items, and monitor progress. If you like, you can distinguish between "bottom-up" and "top-down" planning. Conflicts between top-down and bottom-up values are flagged. To monitor work progress Track+ uses the earned value method.

  • The planning process in Track+
    To plan projects in Track+ you structure all planable items in a hierarchy and enter planned values for the amount of work, and start and end dates for the schedule.
  • Bottom-up and top-down
    In many organizations planning is a recursive process, with desired deadlines and budgets coming from the top, and estimated expenses and delivery dates coming from those that actually do the work.
  • Planning with baselines
    A plan evolves over time and the deviations have to be monitored. With baselines you can keep various versions of a plan and compare the dates and budgets of one version with that of another version.
  • The Earned Value Method
    Earned Value Management (EVM) is a technique for measuring project progress in an objective manner. Inside Track+, EVM is being used to compare planned work to work already spent, giving a conservative and early indication of schedule and expense deviations.
  • Managing work and cost
    The Track+ system handles all expenses in terms of „work“ and „costs“. Work and cost are being booked towards „accounts“.
